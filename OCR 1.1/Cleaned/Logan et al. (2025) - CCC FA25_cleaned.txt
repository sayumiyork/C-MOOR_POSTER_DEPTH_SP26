SECTION: ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The ANCE gene plays a role in processing biological active peptides. In
this project we focused on the Drosophila Melanogaster ANCE gene the
fly ortholog of the human ACE gene. We analyzed four ANCE paralogs
-ANCE 2, ANCE 3, ANCE 4, ANCE 5and examined their differentially
expression patterns. Across different midgut regions, we observed distinct
expression patterns. Within the posterior adult midgut, there 1s a high
ANCE gene expression of the Epithelium enterocyte indicating potential
activity related to the bloodstream. Our results suggest that studying
ANCE gene expression and functional studies may offer insights into
cardiovascular-related pathways and potential therapeutic directions..

SECTION: INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Cardiovascular disorders are a prominent and widespread disease
caused by genetic predispositions and environmental conditions.
The ACE gene found within humans 1s an angiotensin converting
enzyme, which 1s a critical regulator of blood pressure and fluid
balance. This sparked an interest to research the ANCE gene
within Drosophila melanogaster to find a correlation between the
ACE gene and cardiovascular disorders. Drosophila melanogaster is a model organism and by further
investigating this gene we could relate our findings to the ACE
gene within humans. The ACE gene, which regulates blood
pressure and cardiovascular function in humans, has functional
analogs in Drosophila. These analogs help us study conserved
functions in a simpler system. The fruit fly 1s a powerful tool for
understanding the genetic basis of cardiovascular diseases. Using the ANCE gene expressed in the Drosophila, it can be
determined if the ANCE gene is differentially expressed.
Specifically, we analyzed four paralogs of the ANCE geneANCE 2, ANCE 3, ANCE 4, ANCE 5, and found that only the
ANCE and ANCE 3 genes were differentially expressed in a
specific region. This demonstrated a potential functional link
between the ACE gene and the ANCE gene. To better understand
this correlation, single cell RNA sequence data graphs (FIGURE
1.) were uploaded via SciServer which indicated the specific
regions of expression. These regions were within the post interior
region of the midgut highlighting the epithelium enterocyte. Our
research focused heavily on the areas that showed differential
expression within the fruit fly and how these findings might
inform understanding of similar processes in human
development. Since the ACE gene 1s a regulator of fluidity and
the bloodstream, we further analyzed and researched whether or
not cardiovascular disorders could relate to the lack of the ACE
gene expression.

SECTION: DISCUSSION
================================================================================

Our results suggest that certain ANCE genes in Drosophila
melanogaster show region-specific expression patterns in the
midgut, particularly ANCE and ANCE-3, which are active in the
posterior midgut and epithelial enterocytes. These findings
indicate that the ANCE gene family may play a localized role in
peptide processing or fluid balance within the fly’s digestive
system.While the human ACE gene 1s a well-known regulator of
blood pressure and cardiovascular function, we cannot directly
conclude from our Drosophila data that ANCE genes affect
human hypertension or obesity. However, by using Drosophila as
a model organism, we can explore conserved biological pathways
that might contribute to cardiovascular health. Our research
highlights how studies in fruit flies can offer insights into gene
families related to human health, including the potential roles of
ACE-related genes in regulating fluid balance and cardiovascular
function. Future work could include functional experiments to test how
manipulating ANCE expression in Drosophila affects
physiology, providing a foundation for exploring therapeutic
targets in human cardiovascular disease.

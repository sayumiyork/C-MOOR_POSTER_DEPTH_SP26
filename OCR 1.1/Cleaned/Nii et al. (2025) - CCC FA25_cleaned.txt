SECTION: INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Iron is an essential micronutnent involved in redox chemistry, mitochondrial
function, and cellular metabolism. However, elevated iron levels can disrupt
oxidative balance and alter key metabolic pathways. One enzyme potentially
affected by iron status is acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC), the rate limiting
enzyme in de novo fatty acid synthesis (1). ACC generates malonyl-Col, 2
critical substrate for fatty acid elongation and a metabolic signal that
suppresses fatty acd oxidation. A second key metabolic enzyme, ATP citrate
lyase), produces cytosolic acetyl-CoA upstream of ACC and serves as a major
entry point linking carbohydrate metabolism to lipid synthesis. While
mammalian studies show that iron overload increases ACC and promotes
liped accumulation, this relationship has not been examined in Drosophila
melanogaster, a genetically tractable model organism widely used to study
metabolism. Because many metabolic pathways are conserved between flies
and mammals, Drosophila provides an ideal system to test whether the iron
to ACC to lipid synthesis axis represents a conserved regulatory mechanism.
This project investigates how elevated iron influences ACC and ATPCL
expression and lipid storage in Drosophila. We hypothesize that iron exposure
increased ACC activity and downstream fatty acid synthesis leading to greater
triglycende (TAC) accumulation, while ATPCL may serve as a companson
gene to determine whether iron regulates lipid related enzymes broadly or in
a gene specific manner. In mammals, metabolic stress such as high-fat diet
alters ACC activity, and pharmacological ACC inhibition improves
obesity-related phenotypes by reducing tnglycende accumulation and
restoring metabolic balance. These findings highlight ACC as a flexable
metabolic control point. Because iron overload generates oxidative stress,
another form of metabolic stress, we hypothesized that elevated iron may
similarly modulate ACC expression in Drosophila, altering lipid metabolism
and energy storage, and that ATPCL expression will help reveal whether this
response is targeted or global at the level of lipid metabolic enzymes.

SECTION: DISCUSSION
================================================================================

Our results demonstrate that Acetyl-Col carboxylase (ACC) expression is
strongly enriched in the Fe region of the Drosophila midgut. a segment
associated with iron absorption and metal handling. This region specific
elevation suggests that iron availability may selectively enhance ACC
expression, consistent with mammalian studies in which iron overload
increases ACC activity and promotes liped accumulation (2). In contrast, ATP
citrate lyase (ATPCL), an upstream acetyl-Col producing enzyme not
known to be iron responsive, displayed relatively uniform expression across
the midgut and did not show a similar Fe specific increase. The divergent
patterns between ACC and ATPCL indicate that iron does not broadly
upregulate all lipid metabolic enzymes but instead appears to modulate a
key regulatory step in fatty acid synthesis. The Fe specific upregulation of
ACC supports our hypothesis that iron influences lipogenic pathways in
Drosophila by enhancing ACC expression and potentially increasing
malonyl-Coa production (3). Because ACC is the committed step in de novo
fatty acid synthesis, elevated ACC levels in iron rich tissues may contribute
to increased lipid storage and metabolic remodeling. These findings
suggest a conserved iron dependent regulatory mechanism affecting ACC
across species and highlight the fly midgut as a useful system for dissecting
micronutrient drive metabolic changes. Future studies could pair regional
gene expression with direct trighyceride (TAC) quantification or assess
additional downstream genes to further validate the mechanistic link
between iron exposure and pid accumulation
